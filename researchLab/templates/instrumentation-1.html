    Liquid chromatography-mass spectrometry

    Currently, the OSL builds or supports experiments around the analytical instrument Liquid chromatography-mass spectrometry (LC-MS) which is suitable for the sensitive analysis, extraction, and detection of elements in a complex sample. LC-MS is also appropriate for metabolomics because of its good coverage of a wide range of chemicals

    Context model of the LC-MS


    LC-MS is also appropriate for metabolomics because of its good coverage of a wide range of chemicals.[3] This tandem technique can be used to analyze biochemical, organic, and inorganic compounds commonly found in complex samples of environmental and biological origin. Therefore, LC-MS may be applied in a wide range of sectors including biotechnology, environment monitoring, food processing, pharmaceutical, agrochemical, and cosmetic industries. We included a structured process to capture scientific workflows at the conceptual level that allows workflows to be documented efficiently resulting in a concise model of the flow and implementations and providing insight into the scientific process itself.

    The context model is drawn for the LC-MS which consists of different kinds of a component called processes. A process in the context model represents a high-level activity that must be done to complete the experiment. The context model for the process of LC-MS is represented.


    The Supplier-input-producer-output-consumer (SIPOC) model is developed based on the supplier–producer–customer chain, a proven modeling technique used in continuous process improvement. The SIPOC model was chosen as it allows the data and process flow to be captured using a single diagram notation. SIPOC model is represented for LC-MS. As shown in the model, the top-level process, of Analyse sample, collects the sample from the user. The siderophore spiking, Chemical extractions, handling of LC-MS, properties, and procedures all come from different sources as well as information deposited in the reference literature. All of this data is required by the Analyze Sample process to produce a final result, which is composed of both the unrefined, resultant data as well as the refined solution for publication and deposition.

    SIPOC Model for LC-MS


    The process of capturing and designing the workflows for the analysis of biomolecules using LC-MS highlighted several resulting benefits. The modeling process itself caused the spectroscopist to gain a more comprehensive understanding of the steps of the experiment process and their relationships to one another. The process of determining the context, SIPOC, and control flow models permitted the spectroscopist to focus on the experiment process at various levels of abstraction and from various viewpoints, allowing the spectroscopist to gain a new understanding of how the steps in the experiment are related. This understanding has the potential to lead to new and hopefully more-efficient approaches to conducting experiments.
