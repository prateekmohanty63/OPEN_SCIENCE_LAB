With significant growth, usage, and awareness in handling the internet regardless of distance, time, and age factors. The proposal brings an exciting and promising opportunity for society to access Open Science Laboratories (OSLs) to conduct real experiments at a physical location via the internet and fetch real-time results for their benefit.OSL will be a fully aimed to build in fully automated mode, the user sample will be sent to the location, on receiving it the user will be notified with a tracking ID and the user can customize the parameters regarding the process they intend to. The lab provides the flexibility to set the quality of the reagents, thermal setting, reaction time, and so on. In a wider spectrum, this technology allows the user to deploy the equipment from a simple blood diagnostic test to a laborious procedure where the user is trying out to evaluate the microbes in the soil or figuring out a plant leaf that can be a potential element for the discovery of a drug. The geographically flexible OSL will be equipped with the analytical instruments (sequencer, monitor, centrifuge, etc.,) in its designated bay, and the sample will be sent to a test or a series of tests involving one or more equipment. The travel channel of the sample from one bay to the other will be automated by conveyor belts assisted by a manipulator for placing the sample on its domicile. The user can view the response/reaction of every instrument that the sample sent in. The user can repeat the same process with a different reagent or temperature and one can use the data for further exploration. This RL technology gives colossal flexibility for an individual to have instant access to the equipment and encourage them for their course of the invention or discovery or simple diagnostic results. The upshots of the technology will gear up the discovery rate in the country and in the process of setting up, major research fragments, in terms of design and algorithms will be developed. The main focus of this OSL is to : With the increase in the growth of connecting technologies, it is a promising technology or a platform to access lab equipment amidst a routine schedule. At the start of this, there would be a huge interest in the diagnostic instruments companies, and the automation field to automate the end-to-end process with the use of conveyors and manipulators. Cloud-based and automated labs will provide the following benefits:




  1. Access: Make the scientific research equipment available to researchers across all universities and research institutes in India – not just Tier 1 institutes with big budgets and large teams.
  2. Collaboration: make it possible for remote researchers to collaborate and run shared experiments. Make it easier for collaborators to enhance experimental setups suggested by other researchers and make rapid progress.
  3. Replication: Make it easier for researchers to replicate experimental setup and results reported by other researchers. This can help to alleviate the replication crisis.
  4. Citizen science: Make it easier for independent researchers to run custom experiments based on their hypotheses.
