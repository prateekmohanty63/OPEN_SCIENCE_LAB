OSL will be a fully aimed to build in fully automated mode, the user sample will be sent to the location, on receiving it the user will be notified with a tracking ID and the user can customize the parameters regarding the process they intend to. The lab provides the flexibility to set the quality of the reagents, thermal setting, reaction time, and so on. In a wider spectrum, this technology allows the user to deploy the equipment from a simple blood diagnostic test to a laborious procedure where the user is trying out to evaluate the microbes in the soil or figuring out a plant leaf that can be a potential element for the discovery of a drug.

The travel channel of the sample from one bay to the other will be automated by conveyor belts assisted by a manipulator for placing the sample on its domicile. The user can view the response/reaction of every instrument that the sample sent in. The user can repeat the same process with a different reagent or temperature and one can use the data for further exploration. This RL technology gives colossal flexibility for an individual to have instant access to the equipment and encourage them for their course of the invention or discovery or simple diagnostic results. The upshots of the technology will gear up the discovery rate in the country and in the process of setting up, major research fragments, in terms of design and algorithms will be developed. OUR OBJECTIVES

OPEN SCIENCE LAB

01

Aims to widen the access to the laboratories from their location.

02

This lab plans to build around bio-chemistry-based analysis experiments which are currently handled only by the lab technician due to the high maintenance of the types of equipment and also it needs a strong knowledge of instrument handling.

03

More efficient R&D spending by providing wider access to equipment

04

Make a broad spectrum of latest and expensive equipment (as well as old and “long-tail” equipment) accessible to researchers in academia (not just Tier 1 universities) as well as to researchers in private & public-sector companies and to independent researchers (and, thereby, promote citizen science)

05

Enable researchers to design and run actual experiments in Science (Physics, Chemistry, Biology), Engineering (automotive, space, etc.), and Technology (pharma, medicine, etc.)

06

Higher utilization of equipment by aggregating demand across a wider set of researchers; also, lower overheads due to centralized administration of equipment (including upgrading drivers, handling repairs, managing redundancy, etc.); this will free up researchers to focus on research instead of managing labs (and dealing with high turnover of lab technicians, etc.)
